About

How I Think

I'm Fatima, a Creative Technologist and Interactive Experience Designer. I build things that let people feel their way into ideas that are usually only explained to them — through interaction, motion, and systems that respond.

Philosophy

I think most complex ideas aren't hard because they're complicated — they're hard because they're only ever described, never experienced. I'd rather build something you can move through than write another paragraph about it. Interaction, used carefully, does the explaining for me.

Not every idea needs motion. The ones that do get it; the rest stay quiet.

How I Approach a Project

I don't start with a finished concept — I figure out the idea as I build. A project usually begins as a small technical question rather than a plan: what happens if particles chase gravity toward a cursor? What does a genome look like if you can drag its traits mid-simulation? The actual idea reveals itself once something is on screen and responding. Prototyping isn't a phase before the real work — it is the real work.

What I'm Drawn To

I'm drawn to work at the edge of physics, biology, and systems that usually stay invisible — gravity, evolution, chemical signaling, the mechanics of thought itself. Lately that's meant simulating things most people only ever see explained in a textbook: a black hole's lensing, a genome under selection pressure, a flower's chemical warning to its neighbors.

How I Learn

I follow a curiosity until it becomes a project. If I don't understand how something works — gravitational lensing, DNA base pairing, how a neural net actually propagates a signal — building a working version of it is how I get there. The understanding and the finished piece usually arrive at the same time.
